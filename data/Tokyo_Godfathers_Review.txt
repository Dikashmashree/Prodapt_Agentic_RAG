REVIEW:
Satoshi Kon’s Tokyo Godfathers is a vibrant and deeply humane animated film that reimagines the "foundling" narrative within modern Tokyo. Unlike his psychological works, this film embraces a grounded and emotionally rich storytelling approach.

The story follows three marginalized individuals—a middle-aged alcoholic, a transgender woman, and a teenage runaway—who discover an abandoned baby on Christmas Eve. This event sets them on a journey that forces them to confront their past and redefine their sense of belonging.

The film explores themes of redemption and chosen family, portraying characters often ignored by society. Through their care for the child, they regain dignity and purpose.

Visually, the film presents a gritty yet detailed version of Tokyo, avoiding the polished aesthetics typical of animation. Humor and emotional depth coexist, making the characters feel authentic and relatable.
---
VERDICT:
A heartfelt and unconventional urban tale that blends realism with optimism, highlighting human resilience and empathy.
